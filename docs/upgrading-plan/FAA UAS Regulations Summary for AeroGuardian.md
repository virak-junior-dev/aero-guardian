# FAA UAS Regulations Summary for AeroGuardian

## 1. Part 107: Small Unmanned Aircraft Systems (sUAS)
*   **§107.51 Operating limitations for small unmanned aircraft:**
    *   **Altitude:** Maximum 400 feet above ground level (AGL), unless within 400 feet of a structure.
    *   **Speed:** Maximum 100 mph (87 knots).
    *   **Visibility:** Minimum 3 statute miles from the control station.
*   **§107.31 Visual line of sight (VLOS) aircraft operation:**
    *   The remote pilot in command (RPIC) must be able to see the aircraft throughout the entire flight.
*   **§107.37 Operation near aircraft; right-of-way rules:**
    *   UAS must yield right of way to all other aircraft.
*   **§107.39 Operation over human beings:**
    *   Prohibits flight over people unless they are directly participating or under a covered structure.

## 2. Part 89: Remote Identification (Remote ID)
*   **§89.110 Remote identification broadcast requirements:**
    *   Requires UAS to broadcast identification and location information (Serial Number, Latitude/Longitude, Altitude, Velocity, Time Mark).
*   **§89.115 Means of compliance:**
    *   Standard Remote ID (built-in) or Remote ID Broadcast Module (add-on).

## 3. Key Violations for AeroGuardian Detection
*   **Altitude Violation:** Flight > 400ft AGL (§107.51).
*   **VLOS Violation:** Flight at distances/altitudes where VLOS is physically impossible (§107.31).
*   **Airspace Violation:** Flight in restricted or controlled airspace without authorization.
*   **Near-Miss/Collision Hazard:** Close proximity to manned aircraft (§107.37).
